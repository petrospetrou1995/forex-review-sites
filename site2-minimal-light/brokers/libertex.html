Libertex Review

A broker/platform focused on a streamlined web and mobile experience. Always confirm fees and the regulated entity for your country.

Quick verdict

Best for traders who prefer a clean web/mobile platform and want a simple interface. Review total costs carefully.

4.9/5

Example score for comparison UI + schema.

Regulation (example)

CySEC (entity and availability depend on region). Verify the license on the official regulator site.

Platforms

Web platform and mobile apps (platform lineup can vary by region).

Pros

  • Clean, beginner-friendly interface
  • Works well on web and mobile
  • Good for traders who prefer simplicity

Cons

  • Fee structure can be confusing—verify the full schedule
  • Platform options may be more limited vs MT4/MT5-only brokers
  • Conditions vary by entity/region

Who it’s best for

Traders who want a straightforward web/mobile experience and value a clean interface over extensive platform customization.

What to check before signing up

  • Regulated entity and investor protections available to you
  • All trading costs and non-trading fees (inactivity, withdrawals, etc.)
  • Platform features you need (order types, charting, alerts)

FAQ

  • Is Libertex regulated?
    Libertex operates under specific entities. Verify the exact entity and license for your country on the regulator’s register.
  • What are the main Libertex fees?
    Main costs can include spreads, commissions, and swaps depending on instrument and account type. Confirm the full fee schedule.
  • How can I test withdrawals safely?
    Complete KYC first, deposit a small amount, then withdraw back to the same method and record processing time before scaling.
