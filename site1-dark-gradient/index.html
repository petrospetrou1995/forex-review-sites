Trading Strategies

Proven trading strategies used by professional traders. Learn scalping, day trading, swing trading, and position trading techniques.

Scalping Strategy

Learn how to profit from small price movements by opening and closing positions quickly. This strategy requires discipline, fast execution, and low spreads. Perfect for active traders who can monitor the markets closely.

Guidance: trade during peak liquidity sessions and keep execution costs in focus.

  • Focus on highly liquid pairs and tight spreads
  • Predefine entry/exit rules before sessions
  • Keep risk per trade very small
  • Avoid trading during major news spikes

Day Trading Strategy

Master the art of day trading by opening and closing positions within the same trading day. Learn entry and exit signals, time management, and how to avoid overtrading.

Guidance: build a daily plan with clear setups and stop trading after your limit.

  • Trade around high-volume sessions
  • Avoid holding positions overnight
  • Track daily loss limits
  • Journal outcomes to refine your edge

Swing Trading Strategy

Capture medium-term price movements by holding positions for several days or weeks. This strategy is ideal for traders who cannot monitor markets constantly but want to take advantage of larger price swings.

Guidance: confirm trends on higher timeframes and use wider stops to allow volatility.

  • Use higher timeframes to filter noise
  • Set wider stops to allow volatility
  • Review positions once per day
  • Scale out at key swing levels

Trend Following Strategy

Learn to identify and follow market trends for consistent profits. This strategy uses technical indicators to determine trend direction and enter trades in the direction of the prevailing trend.

Guidance: align with the dominant trend and avoid counter-trend entries.

  • Combine trend filters with price structure
  • Scale in only after confirmation
  • Use trailing stops to protect gains
  • Let winners run and cut losers quickly

Breakout Trading Strategy

Profit from price breakouts above resistance or below support levels. Learn how to identify valid breakouts, set appropriate stop-losses, and manage positions during volatile market conditions.

Guidance: look for consolidation before the break and confirm with volume or retest.

  • Wait for volume or retest confirmation
  • Place stops beyond key levels
  • Avoid major news spikes
  • Use targets based on range height

Carry Trade Strategy

Earn interest rate differentials by buying high-yield currencies and selling low-yield currencies. Understand how interest rates affect currency values and how to implement this long-term strategy.

Guidance: favor stable macro trends and monitor policy shifts closely.

  • Prefer stable macro environments
  • Monitor central bank policy shifts
  • Use wider risk limits for long holds
  • Review swaps and funding costs weekly

FAQ

Direct answers to common questions about choosing brokers, costs, leverage, and safety.

How do I choose the best forex broker for my country?

Start by verifying the broker‚Äôs exact licensed entity for your region (e.g., FCA/ASIC/CySEC). Then compare total costs (spread + commissions + swaps) and test withdrawals and support with a small amount before scaling.

What costs matter most when comparing brokers?

Compare all-in trading costs: spreads, commissions (especially on raw accounts), and swaps/financing if you hold positions overnight. Also consider deposit/withdrawal fees and execution quality during volatile sessions.

Why can a broker show 0.0 pips but still be expensive?

Zero or near-zero spreads are often paired with per-trade commissions and sometimes higher swaps. Always look at the combined (all-in) cost for your typical trade size and holding time.

Does high leverage mean a broker is better?

Not necessarily. Higher leverage increases risk and can amplify losses. It‚Äôs better to prioritize regulation, transparent fees, and reliable withdrawals, then choose leverage that matches your risk plan.

What should I test before depositing a large amount?

Test onboarding, platform stability, spreads during your trading hours, support response, and at least one small deposit and withdrawal to confirm processing times and any fees.
