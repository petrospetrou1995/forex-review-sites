FAQ

Direct answers to common questions about choosing brokers, costs, leverage, and safety.

How do I choose the best forex broker for my country?

Start by verifying the broker‚Äôs exact licensed entity for your region (e.g., FCA/ASIC/CySEC). Then compare total costs (spread + commissions + swaps) and test withdrawals and support with a small amount before scaling.

What costs matter most when comparing brokers?

Compare all-in trading costs: spreads, commissions (especially on raw accounts), and swaps/financing if you hold positions overnight. Also consider deposit/withdrawal fees and execution quality during volatile sessions.

Why can a broker show 0.0 pips but still be expensive?

Zero or near-zero spreads are often paired with per-trade commissions and sometimes higher swaps. Always look at the combined (all-in) cost for your typical trade size and holding time.

Does high leverage mean a broker is better?

Not necessarily. Higher leverage increases risk and can amplify losses. It‚Äôs better to prioritize regulation, transparent fees, and reliable withdrawals, then choose leverage that matches your risk plan.

What should I test before depositing a large amount?

Test onboarding, platform stability, spreads during your trading hours, support response, and at least one small deposit and withdrawal to confirm processing times and any fees.
